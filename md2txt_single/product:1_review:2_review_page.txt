#####  _3.0 out of 5 stars_ Not Worth the Hype ⟨109⟩
Reviewed in India on 20 June 2025
Colour: Black __Size: 128 GB⟨110⟩ __Verified Purchase⟨100⟩
This is my first iPhone, and it has been a slight disappointment so far.  
Many of the most useful Android features are missing in iOS. Here are a few:  
1. Lack of a universal back button/gesture (which makes navigation difficult when compared to Android).  
2. Inability to adjust the volume levels differently for media, calls, notifications and alarms.  
3. Lack of information about which all devices are using your phone's hotspot.  
4. A very limited stock keyboard which does not even have a number row (sometimes even the "." button goes missing).  
5. Lack of features in notifications (eg: "mark as read" option for a WhatsApp notification is missing in iOS).  
6. Limited AI capabilities.  
  
Plus, the battery life is not good enough for a phone in this range.  
  
Few of the positives are:  
1. Good camera/video capabilities.  
2. Smooth experience (probably because of the A18 chip and optimizations).  
3. Few useful stock iOS apps (eg: Safari)  
4. Good integration with MacBook and iPad (for example you could answer your iPhone calls on your MacBook and iPad).  
  
Overall, I would say that the iPhone is not worth its hype, because the negatives outweigh the few positives. In my opinion, you would be better off with a similarly priced Android device.  
  
The iPhone popularity is a good case study for marketing and human psychology :)
